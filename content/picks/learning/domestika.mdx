Domestika is an online creative learning platform that produces every course in-house — working practitioners commissioned and filmed by Domestika's own production team, which is what keeps the catalogue at one consistent register rather than the open-upload lottery. The disciplines that matter here are photography and post-production: digital, portrait, and fine-art photography, retouching, and the Lightroom and Capture One end of the workflow. The wider catalogue runs through illustration, design, 3D, writing, and most of the creative trades, across sixteen disciplines, with new courses added every week.

Access works two ways. A course can be bought outright and kept permanently, including its exercises and project community; or Domestika Plus, the yearly subscription, opens a rotating free-to-watch library plus credits exchangeable for any course in the catalogue. The mechanic worth knowing before clicking through: the heavily discounted single-course offers are entry points into a Plus trial that converts to an annual subscription if it isn't cancelled in time — the listed course is the cheap part, the conversion is the actual transaction. Certificates come with completed purchased courses, downloadable and linked back to the project work.
