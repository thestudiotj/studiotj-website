The Colorsound Tremolo was a British analog pedal manufactured by Sola Sound in the 1970s — wide modulation range and a circuit design distinct from the contemporary Fender and Vox tremolo voicings. Martinic models the pedal's analog signal path rather than the modulation curve, which keeps the original's behaviour at the extremes of the depth and speed controls.
