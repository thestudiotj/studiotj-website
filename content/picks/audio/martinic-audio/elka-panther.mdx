The Elka Panther 300 was a portable transistor combo organ manufactured in Italy through the 1970s — a stage instrument for keyboard players touring without a full Hammond. Two manuals, bass pedals, and a percussion register, all in a foldaway chassis. Martinic's plugin keeps the pitch instability and tonal character of the original analog electronics intact rather than smoothing them out.
