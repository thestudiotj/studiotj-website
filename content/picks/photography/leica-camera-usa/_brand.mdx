Leica Camera is a German optical instruments company headquartered in Wetzlar. They manufacture cameras, lenses, binoculars, and sport optics. The camera lines — M-series rangefinders, Q-series compacts, SL mirrorless — share a commitment to full-frame sensors and manual-first operation. The lenses are ground and assembled in-house, with tolerances that reflect a century of optical manufacturing.

The tension Leica owns is straightforward: the price is high, the build justifies it, and the heritage is real rather than performed. The M-system has been in continuous production since 1954. That is not a marketing position — it is a supply chain and a customer base that makes replacement parts available for sixty-year-old cameras. The quality is not legacy — it is the current production standard.
